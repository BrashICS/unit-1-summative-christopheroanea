History of Ken Thompson

Life

Ken Thompson is an American Computer Scientist born on February 4th in New Orleans. Ken Thompson was always fascinated with arithemetic when he was younger. Thompson would often do math problems for fun and found enjoyment within it. In 1965, Ken Thomspon received his Bachelor's of Science and eventually received his Doctors in 1966. He received both of these degree's within electrical engineering and computer sciences. Right after receiving his doctors, Ken Thompson Joined Bell labs where he would spend most of his career.

B programming was a progamming language developped in 1969 by Ken Thompson and Dennis Ritchie. Surprisingly, B programming was a non typing programming language and merely relied on the computers memory to function and was mainly used for system programming. Unfortunately, B programming is not used much any more to this day but still played a big part in Ken Thompson's career since it was the first programming language he created. The creation of the B programming language also allowed Dennis Ritchie and Ken Thomspon to build off of it eventually creating the C programming language.
